My Terrace Garden Blog

Welcome to my terrace garden! I have always dreamed of having a small green space at home where I could relax, enjoy the fresh air, and watch plants grow. Our terrace gave me the perfect opportunity to start my garden. Initially, I planted only a few plants, but over time, it has transformed into a beautiful garden filled with a variety of plants. Every plant has its own charm, and taking care of them has been a wonderful learning experience.

Mother of Thousands

The Mother of Thousands is a fascinating succulent. Tiny baby plants grow along the edges of its leaves, and seeing them appear is always exciting. This plant multiplies quickly, giving the terrace a lush and green look. It is very easy to care for and does not require frequent watering. I love watching the small plantlets grow into new plants over time, making the garden lively and dynamic.

Gomphrena

Gomphrena, also known as Globe Amaranth, is a beautiful flowering plant with round, vibrant blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white. It grows well in pots and sunny areas of the terrace. The flowers last a long time and are often used for decoration and dried flower arrangements. This plant adds bright colors to the garden and attracts butterflies, making the terrace lively and cheerful.

Sunflower

Sunflowers are bright, cheerful, and full of energy. Their tall stems and vibrant yellow petals instantly uplift the terrace. They require plenty of sunlight, and taking care of them has taught me patience and attention. I love seeing them bloom in the morning, swaying gently in the breeze. They attract bees and butterflies, adding even more life to the garden. Every sunflower feels like a little sun that brings happiness to my day.

Snake Plant

The Snake Plant is extremely easy to maintain. Even if I occasionally forget to water it, it remains healthy. It is also known to purify the air, which makes the terrace a healthier place to relax. Its tall, upright leaves add a structured and neat look to the garden. I appreciate how this plant grows steadily without much care, making it perfect for busy days when I cannot tend to the garden.

Butterfly Pea Flower

The Butterfly Pea produces stunning blue flowers that are both beautiful and useful. I use its flowers to make tea, which is not only refreshing but also healthy. This plant grows well in pots and spreads quickly, adding color and life to the terrace. It attracts butterflies and small birds, creating a lively environment. Watching these visitors interact with the flowers is a pure joy and makes me feel connected to nature.

Insulin Plant

The Insulin Plant is one of the most important plants in my garden due to its medicinal properties. It is very easy to grow and adapts quickly to the sunny corners of the terrace. Its fresh green leaves make the garden feel alive and healthy. I water it regularly and watch it grow steadily, which gives me satisfaction and peace. This plant also serves as a reminder of how nature provides us with remedies if we take care of it.

Rose Plant

The Rose Plant is the crown jewel of my terrace garden. Its vibrant blooms and delightful fragrance make the space feel like a little paradise. Watching the roses open every morning is a calming and uplifting experience. Taking care of it teaches patience, as roses need attention to thrive. I feel proud when the flowers bloom beautifully, adding color and elegance to my terrace.

Gardening on the terrace has been a journey of learning, patience, and joy. Each plant tells its own story, and taking care of them makes me feel responsible and connected to nature. I hope my blog inspires you to start your own garden, no matter how small, and enjoy the beauty and happiness that plants bring into your life.
